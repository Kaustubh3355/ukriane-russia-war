Historical factors

On 24 February 2022, Russia invaded and occupied parts of Ukraine in a major escalation of the Russo-Ukrainian War, which had begun in 2014. The invasion has resulted in tens of thousands of deaths on both sides, and instigated Europe's largest refugee crisis since World War II.

Geopolitical factors

The geopolitical impact of the conflict in Ukraine is significant. The world has been watching the events unfold in Ukraine with apprehension, dismay and fear, and the attack has shaken the foundations of our international peace and security system.

It has caused a large humanitarian crisis and is wreaking havoc on an already fragile global economy, which was just managing to slowly recover from the disruption caused by the pandemic.
